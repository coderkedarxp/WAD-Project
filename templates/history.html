History of Pune

2015

Pune Metro Inauguration

Pune's infrastructure underwent significant development with the inauguration of the Pune Metro rail project. The metro project aimed to alleviate traffic congestion and improve connectivity within the city, providing a modern and efficient mode of public transportation for Pune residents.

2010

Bomb Blast Tragedy

Pune faced a series of bomb blasts in February 2010, targeting various locations in the city, including the German Bakery in Koregaon Park. The coordinated attacks resulted in several casualties and injuries, highlighting security concerns and the threat of terrorism in Pune.

1990

IT Hub Emergence

By the 1990s, Pune had established itself as one of India's major IT and automotive manufacturing hubs, attracting investments and industries from around the world. The city witnessed rapid urban development, infrastructure growth, and a burgeoning IT sector, solidifying its position as a leading center for technology, innovation, and entrepreneurship in India.

1961

Urbanization Boom

In 1961 AD, Pune experienced rapid urbanization and industrial growth, emerging as a prominent educational and industrial center in India. The establishment of institutions such as the National Chemical Laboratory furthered Pune's reputation as a hub for education, research, and innovation, contributing to its economic development and growth.

1942

Quit India Movement

During the Quit India Movement in 1942 AD, Pune emerged as a hotbed of resistance against British colonial rule. The city witnessed widespread protests, demonstrations, and acts of civil disobedience, with many prominent leaders and activists from Pune actively participating in the movement, demanding an end to British rule and India's independence.

1894

New English School

In 1894 AD, the New English School was established in Pune, marking a significant milestone in the city's educational history. The school played a pivotal role in promoting education in vernacular languages and became a center for intellectual and cultural exchange, contributing to the growth of the Indian freedom movement.

1857

Rebellion in Pune

Pune played a significant role in the Indian Rebellion of 1857, also known as the First War of Independence, against British colonial rule. The city witnessed widespread protests and uprisings, with many freedom fighters from Pune actively participating in the rebellion, contributing to the fight for India's independence.

1818

British Conquest

In 1818 AD, Pune was captured by the British East India Company after the Third Anglo-Maratha War, marking the end of Maratha rule in the city. This event led to significant changes in Pune's political landscape and paved the way for British colonial administration.

1730

Peshwa Dominance

By the 18th century, Pune had become the stronghold of the Peshwas, who served as the prime ministers of the Maratha Empire. This period saw Pune's transformation into a major political and cultural center, with the Peshwas playing a significant role in its development and governance.

1630

Foundation of Pune

Founded by Chattrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1630 AD, Pune became the capital of the Maratha Kingdom. Under Shivaji's rule, the city flourished as a center of Maratha power, administration, and culture, playing a crucial role in shaping the history of the Maratha Empire.

13th Century

Yadava Rule

During the 13th century AD, Pune came under the control of the Yadava Dynasty, which furthered its development as a cultural and political hub in the region. This period saw the city's emergence as an important center of trade and commerce.

8th Century

Rashtrakuta Settlement

In the 8th century AD, Pune, then known as Punaka Vishaya, witnessed settlement by the Rashtrakuta Dynasty. This marked its early history as a regional center, contributing to its cultural and economic growth in the region.
